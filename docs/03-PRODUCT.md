# REZNO Product Specification

## Purpose

This document defines the REZNO product, its users, core modules, and overall product direction.

It serves as the functional reference for future development.

---

# Product Overview

REZNO is a unified booking and business management platform designed to simplify how customers discover and book services while helping businesses efficiently manage their daily operations.

The platform should provide a seamless experience for both customers and businesses through one integrated ecosystem.

REZNO should never be limited to one industry.

Every feature should be designed to work across multiple business categories.

---

# Product Objectives

The platform aims to:

- Simplify the booking process.
- Reduce administrative work for businesses.
- Improve customer experience.
- Centralize business management.
- Increase operational efficiency.
- Support future expansion.

---

# Customer Experience

Customers should be able to:

- Create an account.
- Discover businesses.
- Search by category or location.
- View business profiles.
- Browse available services.
- Check available appointment times.
- Book appointments.
- Modify or cancel bookings (subject to business policies).
- Receive booking confirmations.
- Receive reminders and notifications.
- Manage their profile.
- View booking history.
- Save favorite businesses.

The customer experience should always remain simple, fast, and intuitive.

---

# Business Experience

Businesses should be able to:

- Register their business.
- Complete onboarding.
- Create one or multiple branches.
- Manage employees.
- Manage working hours.
- Manage services.
- Manage appointment availability.
- Receive bookings.
- Approve or manage bookings when required.
- Manage customer information.
- View reports and statistics.
- Configure business settings.

The dashboard should help businesses operate more efficiently with minimal complexity.

---

# Administration Experience

Platform administrators should be able to:

- Manage users.
- Manage businesses.
- Review reports.
- Handle platform moderation.
- Manage categories.
- Configure platform settings.
- Monitor platform activity.

Administrative tools should focus on platform management rather than business operations.

---

# Core Modules

The initial platform should include:

- Authentication
- User Management
- Business Management
- Branch Management
- Employee Management
- Service Management
- Booking Management
- Schedule Management
- Customer Dashboard
- Business Dashboard
- Admin Dashboard
- Notifications
- Profile Management
- Search
- Settings

Additional modules may be introduced in future versions.

---

# Supported Business Types

The platform should support businesses including but not limited to:

- Beauty Salons
- Barber Shops
- Clinics
- Hospitals
- Restaurants
- Cafés
- Hotels
- Gyms
- Sports Facilities
- Car Rental
- Home Services
- Educational Centers
- Professional Services

The architecture should remain generic enough to support new industries without major redesign.

---

# User Roles

The platform currently includes:

- Customer
- Business Owner
- Business Manager
- Employee
- Administrator
- Super Administrator

Every role should have clearly defined permissions.

---

# Product Principles

Every feature should be:

- Useful
- Easy to understand
- Fast
- Consistent
- Secure
- Scalable

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

---

# MVP Scope

The first production version should focus on:

- Complete authentication.
- Business onboarding.
- Customer dashboard.
- Business dashboard.
- Booking system.
- Calendar and scheduling.
- Notifications.
- Responsive interface.
- Progressive Web App.
- Arabic, Kurdish, and English language support.

Everything else can be introduced in future phases.

---

# Future Expansion

The architecture should allow future additions such as:

- Online payments.
- Loyalty programs.
- Coupons.
- Reviews.
- Multi-location businesses.
- Marketplace improvements.
- Reporting enhancements.
- Integrations with external services.

These features should be added without requiring major architectural changes.

---

# AI Development Note

This document defines the functional scope of REZNO.

When implementing new features:

- Keep the product simple.
- Avoid unnecessary features.
- Build reusable solutions.
- Maintain consistency across the platform.
- If multiple implementation options exist, choose the one that best supports long-term scalability.
